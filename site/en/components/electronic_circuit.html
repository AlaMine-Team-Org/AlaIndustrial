Electronic Circuit

alaindustrial:electronic_circuit
ComponentsImplementedcomponentcircuittier-lv

Summary

The Electronic Circuit is the mod's base crafting component — the "brain" of nearly every early (LV) machine and storage block. It's an item, not a block: you only see it as an ingredient in machine recipes. It's what makes the progression feel real — before you can assemble a machine, you first have to assemble its brain. It's built at a workbench from the mod's metals (silver and nickel) plus quartz and an emerald, so it pushes you to mine and smelt the mod's ores. The circuit itself stores no energy.

Mini-example: smelt some silver and nickel, grab a piece of quartz and an emerald, and assemble a circuit at your workbench. That one circuit is the part every LV machine asks for — make a few and you're ready to craft your Macerator.

Energy

Uses no energy — it's a plain crafting ingredient.

How it's used

A pure intermediate, and the early-game bottleneck: every LV machine and storage block asks for at least one. It crafts into the Generator, the Macerator, the Battery Box and the rest of the early machine line. It later evolves into the Advanced Circuit (for MV and above).

Block Properties

Not applicable (item). Stack: 64.

Recipes

Assembled at a workbench (shaped 3×3): silver ingots in the four corners, nickel ingots top and bottom centre, quartz on the left and right sides, and an emerald as the conducting core in the centre.

SNS
QEQ
SNS

S — silver ingot (×4); N — nickel ingot (×2); Q — quartz (×2); E — emerald (×1).

Progression

A component with no tier of its own. The early crafting bottleneck — every LV machine needs at least one, which forces you to mine and smelt the mod's silver and nickel before you automate. Priced to sting a little, not to block the first machine.

Related

Macerator — the first machine that requires a circuit. Silver and Nickel — the ores you mine for it. Material Forms — the wider ore → dust → ingot chain.
