Extractor

alaindustrial:extractor
MachinesLVImplementedtier-lvprocessing

Summary

The Extractor is an LV machine that pulls useful material out of raw inputs: dyes from flowers, seeds from pumpkin and melon, powder from blaze rods, flint from gravel. It is usually more profitable than the vanilla routes: a flower yields ×2 dye instead of ×1 at the crafting table, a pumpkin yields ×5 seeds instead of ×4. It is part of the basic LV processing chain and a handy node for hopper-fed auto-farms.

Energy

The Extractor only consumes EU/FE and keeps a small buffer for smooth running.

An LV machine — it draws up to 32 EU/FE per tick. Any surplus in the network is not forwarded, the block is not damaged. Faces: EU/FE comes in on five faces; the front face (where the machine "looks") is inert — cables do not connect to it and hoppers do not work through it.

Processing

Each operation costs 240 EU/FE and takes 120 ticks (~6 s). The recipes are ordinary datapack recipes — editable with a resource pack or /reload.

Dyes: most flowers yield ×2 (the crafting table gives ×1), and rich sources (lilac, wither rose) give ×3. Kelp yields ×1 — it has little pigment. Seeds: pumpkin → ×5 (the crafting table gives ×4), melon slice → ×2 (the crafting table gives ×1) — the Extractor is always a little better.

Balance

Base: 2 EU/FE/tick × 120 ticks = 240 EU/FE — the shared default LV-machine "price" (the fallback when a recipe sets no energy). All 14 extraction recipes cost 240 EU/FE each (120 ticks). Numbers are starter values. Profitability over the vanilla routes is the main incentive to build an Extractor: the crafting table gives ×1 dye from a flower, the furnace gives ×1 from cactus/sea pickle; the Extractor gives ×2 (×3 from rich sources). It doesn't break balance — dyes aren't critical to progression, but they give hopper-fed auto-farms a meaningful way to process plant matter.

Visuals

The body is shared with the other LV machines: the front face turns toward the player on placement and lights up while working. The interface window is shared too — an energy bar and two slots, with no dedicated texture of its own.
