Wind Turbine

alaindustrial:wind_mill
GeneratorsLVImplementedtier-lvenergywind

Summary

The Wind Turbine is a simple entry-level (LV) generator: it produces energy (EU/FE) from the wind, no fuel needed. It needs three things to work: a Wooden Rotor in its slot (no rotor — no generation), open sky above the block and free space around the blades (in front of the block, to the sides and a pit below — otherwise the blades hit the neighbouring blocks). The higher you build it and the worse the weather (rain, thunderstorm) — the more energy it makes. Over time the turbine can be upgraded: insert an evolution chip (the same one solar panels use) and it turns into the Sky Mill (stronger at altitude) or the Tempest Mill (stronger in bad weather). The Wind Turbine rewards players who build high and aren't afraid of storms.

Quick start (cheat sheet)

  1. Craft the Wind Turbine and a Wooden Rotor (recipes are at the bottom of the page).
  2. Build a tall tower: below sea level (y = 63) the turbine doesn't work at all; it peaks at y ≈ 128.
  3. Place the block and open it (right-click), insert the rotor into the central slot.
  4. Make sure the sky above the block is open and the blade area (the block in front of the

turbine, one block to its left/right and three below it) contains only air.

  1. Connect a cable to the back wall — the other sides don't output energy.
For reference: one turbine at y ≈ 128 produces 4 EU/FE/t in clear weather and 8 EU/FE/t in a thunderstorm. The turbine has an internal buffer of 4000 EU/FE — if the energy isn't drained by a cable, generation pauses until there is room again.

Turbine not working? Check in this order: is the rotor in its slot → is there a roof above the block → is a block obstructing the blades → is a neighbouring turbine too close → is it calm (near the ground in clear weather the output is 0 — build higher).

Generation

The higher the turbine above sea level and the worse the weather, the more energy it makes. The base grows with altitude: +1 EU/FE/tick per 16 blocks above sea level, up to 4 EU/FE/tick at roughly y ≈ 128. Weather multiplies the base: clear ×1, rain ×1.5, thunder ×2, with an overall cap of 8 EU/FE/tick. Near the ground or under a roof, the turbine is useless.

If something is wrong, the turbine says so in plain language on its screen: "no rotor", "roofed", "blocked", "interference" or "calm". When it's actually generating, the gear indicator lights up.

Blade clearance

Simple rule: the blades spin in the block in front of the turbine and occupy a 2×2 square. Keep clear the block in front of the turbine, one block to its left and right, and three blocks below it (the pit) — otherwise the turbine shows the "Blocked" mode and stops. "Clear" means air, grass, flowers, saplings or a snow layer (anything you can place a block into). Anything with real hardness — stone, glass, leaves, fences, slabs, or the turbine's own support pillar — obstructs. A roof above is caught separately by the open-sky check, so the air above the blades doesn't need to be checked. This logic is shared by all three turbines (T1, Sky, Tempest).

Rotor interference

Simple placement rules for multiple turbines: Facing the same way and standing side by side — leave 1 block of air between them; placed right next to each other their blades collide and both stop. Back to back — can be placed right next to each other. Face to face — need at least 2 blocks between them. Important: both turbines stop, with no exceptions, and their blades disappear from the renderer (so they don't flicker through each other). A neighbour without a rotor causes no interference — but the moment you insert a rotor into it, a running turbine next door stops too. A stationary neighbouring rotor counts just like a spinning one. This logic is also shared by all three turbines. Stop-reason priority: no rotor → roof → obstruction → interference → weather. Obstruction beats weather: even in a full thunderstorm a block in front of the blades (or a foreign rotor in the disc) stops generation — and chip evolution doesn't progress either until the blades spin again. Altitude, sky and weather are recalculated not every tick but roughly once every two seconds; between recalculations the turbine outputs the same rate.

Progression

The basic turbine is the neutral tier-1 form. Insert an evolution chip (shared with solar panels) and let it work under open sky with a rotor installed — it evolves into one of the branches, keeping its stored energy: Solar Evolution Chip → Sky branch: altitude boost. Lunar Evolution Chip → Tempest branch: bad-weather boost.

TierSolar chip (altitude)Lunar chip (storm)
T1Wind Turbine (neutral)Wind Turbine (neutral)

Threshold: 33,600 active ticks (rotor installed + chip + open sky). With continuous clear weather that's about 28 real minutes (roughly 3 in-game days).
