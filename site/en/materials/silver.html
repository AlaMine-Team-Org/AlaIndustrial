Silver Ore

alaindustrial:silver_ore
MaterialsImplementedmaterialmetalsoresworldgen

Summary

Silver is a custom ore metal slated for the electronics role: the material for circuits and components (ore — Phase 0 worldgen; consumption — in later phases/recipes once they're written, e.g. the advanced circuit / MV entry, possibly earlier). Implemented (mirroring /tin): it generates in the world (stone + a deepslate variant), and the ore is mined with a pickaxe into raw_silver (Fortune-scaled drop, ×2–5 by the vanilla copper_ore scheme); Silk Touch yields the ore block itself. Processing follows the shared metal convention (see tin.md as the reference): smelting raw_silver/ore → ingot (furnace, 1:1), or macerating the ore block → 2 dust → 2 ingots (doubling; raw_silver/ingot → 1 dust). No vanilla silver exists, so the ingot is a mod item (alaindustrial:silver_ingot). block_id/item_id family (implemented):

Recipes

Implemented (mirroring /tin). The main processing path runs through raw_silver; ore→ingot furnace recipes are kept as an alternative route: Furnace/electric furnace, raw → ingot (main path): minecraft:smelting/blasting, recipe/silver_ingot_from_smelting_raw_silver.json + a _blasting_ variant; the electric furnace uses recipe/smelting/raw_silver.json (energy: 200) — analogous to raw_copper/raw_iron. Furnace/electric furnace, ore → ingot (kept, parity with vanilla copper): recipe/silver_ingot_from_smelting_silver_ore.json + deepslate/_blasting_ variants, no doubling. Maceration of the ore block → dust (×2): data/alaindustrial/recipe/maceration/silver.json. Maceration of the ingot → dust (×1, no doubling): data/alaindustrial/recipe/maceration/silver_ingot.json (result.count: 1). Smelting dust → ingot (1:1): data/alaindustrial/recipe/smelting/silver_dust.json. Pickaxe → raw_silver (Fortune-scaled), Silk Touch → the block; the vanilla scheme (like copper_ore).

Progression

Tier: none (material). Leads into: electronics — circuits/components (advanced circuit), entry into MV. Downstream recipes arrive later: the exact ingot counts for the advanced circuit / MV entry will come in their own phases. The acquisition path (ore → raw → dust → ingot) is implemented and works. Requires: a working Macerator and a furnace (smelting).

Compatibility

Recipe viewers: JEI, REI, EMI (ore, raw, dust, ingot as ingredients/results).

Balance

Metal doubling (1 ore → maceration → 2 dust → 2 ingots) is the standard incentive to build a macerator. The machine-free path (furnace) stays 1:1 (no doubling); macerating a finished ingot back to dust is also 1:1. This is the largest economy modifier. Density is derived (model §7.2) from projected demand (16 ingots per MV = 8 advanced circuits × 2 silver). The "electronics" role is deliberate. Numbers are starter values.
