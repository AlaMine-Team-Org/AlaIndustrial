Tempered Iron Pickaxe

alaindustrial:tempered_iron_pickaxe
ItemsImplementedtoolpickaxetempered-irontier-lv

Summary

The Tempered Iron Pickaxe is the Ala Industrial mod's first hand-held mining tool. It's crafted like a normal vanilla pickaxe (3× material on top, 2× sticks below) but uses the mod's tempered iron instead of iron_ingot. On every stat it's slightly better than the vanilla iron pickaxe (durability, speed, damage, enchantability), but it does not mine diamond-level blocks (diamond ore, obsidian, ancient debris) — it stays balanced and doesn't devalue the diamond progression. This is the first tool in the mod and sets the pattern for future tools/weapons: in MC 26.2 tools are data-driven Items with a Tool component (the PickaxeItem/Tier/Tiers classes are gone), and the material is described by the ToolMaterial record.

Repair — not a recipe but a tag: data/alaindustrial/tags/item/tempered_iron_tool_materials.json (value alaindustrial:tempered_iron). In the anvil, 1 tempered iron restores some durability — analogous to repairing a vanilla iron pickaxe with an iron ingot via #iron_tool_materials.

Behavior

Mining: mines every block in the minecraft:mineable/pickaxe tag at the material's speed of 7.0. Blocks in the minecraft:incorrect_for_iron_tool tag (diamond level) drop no items and are mined more slowly (the vanilla "wrong tool" penalty) — identical to the iron pickaxe. Combat: damage 4.5, attack speed with the −2.8 modifier (like the iron pickaxe). The slightly higher damage is its only combat edge. Durability: 350 (100 more than iron). Spent on mining and hits, like any tool. Needs no EU/FE — a purely vanilla wear mechanic. Enchanting: enchantmentValue 16 (iron 14) — a material that "catches enchantments a little better". The pickaxe is added to the membership tag #minecraft:pickaxes, so through the enchantable-tag chain (#minecraft:enchantable/{mining,mining_loot,durability}) the enchanting table offers the full vanilla set — see Compatibility. Repair: in the anvil with tempered iron via the repair tag; the XP cost is vanilla.

Progression

Tier: none (a hand-held tool, not tied to the mod's EnergyTier). It slots between the vanilla iron pickaxe (250/6.0/4/14) and the diamond one (1561/8.0/5/10) — by durability and speed it's closer to iron, and it doesn't overlap diamond on block access. It appears in Phase 1, as soon as the player starts smelting iron into tempered iron en masse (via furnace/blast furnace/electric furnace) — a natural upgrade until the first diamond pickaxe.

Compatibility

Recipe viewers: JEI, REI, EMI — as a regular item with a crafting recipe. Enchantments: by design — all the vanilla ones (efficiency, unbreaking, fortune, silk_touch, mending) as the standard pickaxe set. The pickaxe is added to the membership tag #minecraft:pickaxes (data/minecraft/tags/item/pickaxes.json, replace: false). Through the enchantable-tag chain (#minecraft:enchantable/{mining,mining_loot,durability}) the enchanting table offers efficiency/unbreaking/fortune/silk_touch/mending. The minecraft:story/iron_tools advancement triggers on crafting. The reason the tag needs its own JSON: Item.Properties.pickaxe(...) in MC 26.2 sets only the mining Tool component and does not register the item in the tag (verified with javap -c); the ENCHANTABLE component is set via ToolMaterial.applyCommonProperties → Properties.enchantable(enchantmentValue), but the table's filter goes by supported_items → tags, not by the component. Anvil repair works through tempered_iron_tool_materials independently of the membership tags.

Balance

See the table above. Design goal: "a little better than iron, noticeably weaker than diamond, doesn't break the vanilla block-access progression".
