Tempered Iron Tools

alaindustrial:tempered_iron_tools
ItemsImplementedtooltempered-irontier-lv

Summary

The Tempered Iron tools are the Ala Industrial mod's set of five hand tools: the pickaxe, the axe, the hoe, the shovel and the sword. Each is crafted like its vanilla counterpart, but uses the mod's tempered iron instead of iron ingots. Across the board they're slightly better than vanilla iron: 100 more durability, a bit faster at mining, +0.5 damage, and a little better at catching enchantments. The pickaxe, axe, shovel and hoe, though, cannot mine diamond-level blocks (diamond ore, obsidian, ancient debris) — like iron, so the diamond progression isn't devalued. They're a natural upgrade slot between vanilla iron and diamond: they appear as soon as the player starts smelting iron into tempered iron in bulk.

Energy

These are ordinary hand tools — they don't connect to the mod's energy network and don't use EU/FE.

FieldValue
Rolenone (hand-held tools, not tied to the EU/FE system)

Inventory

Items. Stack: 1 each (durability tools). They hold the minecraft:tool and minecraft:max_damage data components and are repaired via the alaindustrial:tempered_iron_tool_materials tag. Don't stack.

Block Properties

Not applicable (items).

Characteristics (balance)

The baseline is the vanilla iron tools. All five share a single tempered-iron material, so only the common parameters are truly shared — durability, mining speed and enchantability. Damage and attack speed are per-tool, as in vanilla. The table below compares each tool's total damage (iron / tempered). The tempered tools keep the same attack speed as iron; the only difference is the material damage bonus (+0.5).

Common to all five: durability 350 (vs iron's 250, +40 %), mining speed 7.0 (vs 6.0), enchantability 16 (vs 14). The mining level is the same as iron, so diamond-level blocks drop nothing. The sword, like the vanilla one, mines cobweb instantly.

Recipes

All five are crafted at a workbench in the vanilla patterns of the corresponding tools, but with tempered iron instead of iron ingots and sticks for the handles: Pickaxe: III / S / S — 3× tempered_iron + 2× stick Axe: II / S / S — 3× tempered_iron + 2× stick (mirrored vanilla pattern) Hoe: II / S / S — 2× tempered_iron + 2× stick (mirrored vanilla pattern) Shovel: I / S / S — 1× tempered_iron + 2× stick Sword: I / I / S — 2× tempered_iron + 1× stick Repair — this is not a separate recipe but a shared repair tag: any of the five tools is repaired in the anvil with one tempered iron.

Behavior

Harvesting (pickaxe/axe/shovel/hoe): mine their materials (stone, wood, dirt, farmland) at the material's speed of 7.0 — slightly faster than iron. Diamond-level blocks (diamond ore, obsidian, ancient debris) drop nothing — exactly like the iron tools. Axe: additionally — bark stripping, lighting wood on right-click, breaking wooden blocks faster. The full vanilla axe behaviour. Hoe: tilling soil and harvesting crops — like the vanilla hoe. Shovel: makes a dirt path and extinguishes campfires — like the vanilla shovel. Sword: damage 6.5 (vs iron's 6.0), mines cobweb instantly, knocks back enemies. Combat: every tool's damage is +0.5 above its iron counterpart (see the table above); attack speed matches iron. Needs no EU/FE — ordinary wear. Durability: 350 (100 more than iron). Spent on mining and hits. Enchanting: enchantability 16 (vs iron's 14) — a material that catches enchantments a little better. The enchanting table offers the full vanilla set for each tool type — see Compatibility. Repair: in the anvil with tempered iron — any of the five tools is fixed with one ingot.

Progression

Tier: none (hand-held tools, not tied to the mod's energy tiers). They slot between the vanilla iron and diamond tools: by durability and speed they're closer to iron, and on block access they don't overlap diamond. They appear as soon as the player starts smelting iron into tempered iron in bulk — a natural upgrade until the first diamond gear.

Compatibility

Recipe viewers: JEI, REI, EMI — as regular items with crafting recipes. Enchantments: all vanilla enchantments for the corresponding tool type are supported. For the harvesting tools (pickaxe/axe/shovel/hoe) that's efficiency/unbreaking/fortune/silk_touch/mending, for the sword sharpness/looting/fire_aspect/unbreaking/mending. The vanilla "Iron Tools" advancement triggers when the pickaxe is crafted.
